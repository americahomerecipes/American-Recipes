Buffalo Sauce: 1/2 cup Frank's RedHot sauce, 1/3 cup unsalted butter melted, 1 tbsp honey

Execution Steps

Follow our professional heat guidelines to ensure perfect moisture and flavor outcomes.

1

Step 1

Preheat oven to 425°F (218°C). Toss dry chicken wings in a bowl with baking powder and salt until lightly coated.

2

Step 2

Line a baking sheet with foil and place a wire rack on top. Arrange wings in a single layer with space between them.

3

Step 3

Bake for 30 minutes, turning once, until skin is crackly, thin, and deep golden brown.

4

Step 4

In a large bowl, whisk Frank's hot sauce, melted butter, and honey together until emulsified.

5

Step 5

Transfer hot cooked wings to the bowl of buffalo sauce and toss gently until completely coated. Serve hot with celery and blue cheese dressing.

Leftover Management & Storage Rules

Preserve your hard culinary work safely! Leftovers are high-quality assets when handled correctly. Keep our strict timeline in mind for peak freshness:

  • ❄️
    Store broths and soups in deep, airtight containers in the refrigerator for up to 3 to 4 days. Cooked cream soups are best eaten within 2 days.
  • ❄️
    Store salads and dressings in separate containers in the refrigerator; dressed salads will become soggy and do not preserve well.

Frequently Asked Culinary Questions

We tackle the most frequent troubles experienced when preparing this traditional meal:

Q: How do I thicken a soup that is too watery?

Make a quick cornstarch slurry (equal parts cold liquid and cornstarch) and whisk it into the boiling broth, or mash a cup of the soup's cooked potatoes/beans and stir them back in.

Q: Why does my salad taste bland even with dressing?

Make sure you dry your greens completely before dressing. Remaining water dilutes the dressing, preventing it from sticking to the leaves.

Q: Can I freeze soups containing milk or cream?

Dairy-heavy soups can break, curdle, and become grainy when frozen and thawed. It is always best to freeze the broth base, and stir in the milk or cream during reheating.

Q: How do I cut the excessive saltiness of a soup?

Add a splash of fresh acid (like lemon juice or apple cider vinegar) to balance the salt. Alternatively, drop in a peeled raw potato and simmer for 15 minutes; it acts as a gentle sponge to absorb excess sodium.

Q: What is the key to a vibrant, fresh-looking dressing?

Always use cold-pressed extra virgin olive oil, high-quality wine vinegar, and thick emulsifiers like Dijon mustard or pure honey to keep oil and acid beautifully unified.
