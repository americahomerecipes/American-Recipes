New York Style Cheesecake

Historical Legacy & Flavor Profile

The rich, dense, and velvety New York Style Cheesecake is a legendary confection that commands absolute respect in the baking world. Unlike lighter European versions that utilize ricotta or cottage cheese, the signature New York style relies on a massive quantity of rich, block-style cream cheese, creamed with sugar, sour cream, and fresh eggs. Pioneered in the Jewish delis of Manhattan in the 1920s—most notably by Arnold Reuben—this style took advantage of local cream cheese manufacturing advances. The cake features a dense, silky interior over a buttery, slightly salty graham cracker crust. Its clean, rich, vanilla-kissed flavor profile is often balanced by a slight lemon tang, making it an decadent yet beautifully rounded dessert.

Ingredients Checklist

Tap on individual ingredients below to check them off as you prepare your workspace.

Crust: 1.5 cups graham cracker crumbs, 1/4 cup sugar, 6 tbsp melted butter
Filling: 4 pkgs (32 oz) cream cheese at room temp, 1.25 cups granulated sugar
1/2 cup sour cream at room temp
4 large eggs at room temp
1 tbsp lemon juice, 2 tsp vanilla extract
3 tbsp all-purpose flour

Execution Steps

Follow our professional heat guidelines to ensure perfect moisture and flavor outcomes.

1

Step 1

Preheat oven to 325°F (163°C). Combine crust ingredients and pack firmly into bottom of a 9-inch springform pan. Bake for 8 minutes, then cool.

2

Step 2

Beat cream cheese and sugar in a large stand-mixer until completely smooth. Add sour cream, lemon juice, flour, and vanilla; beat to integrate.

3

Step 3

Add eggs one at a time, mixing on low speed just until combined (do not over-incorporate air).

4

Step 4

Pour batter onto baked crust. Wrap the outer bottom of springform pan with double layer of heavy alum foil and set inside a roasting pan.

5

Step 5

Pour hot water half-way up the sides of springform pan to make a water bath. Bake for 75-80 minutes until edges are set but center giggles.

6

Step 6

Turn off oven, open oven door slightly and let rest inside for 1 hour. Chill in refrigerator for at least 6 hours before opening.

Frequently Asked Culinary Questions

We tackle the most frequent troubles experienced when preparing this traditional meal:

Q: Why did my cheesecake crack on top?

Cheesecakes crack when baked in a hot, dry oven, or when cooled too rapidly. Baking the cake inside a gentle steam water bath (bain-marie) and letting it cool inside the turned-off oven prevents this issue.

Q: Do all ingredients really need to be at room temperature?

Yes, room-temperature ingredients are absolutely vital! Cold cream cheese will form stubborn lumps that are nearly impossible to smooth out. Warm ingredients blend smoothly, preventing overbeating.

Q: How can I tell if a cheesecake is finished baking?

The 'jiggle test' is key. The outer 2 inches should look firm and matte, but the very center (about 3 inches) should still jiggle slightly like set gelatin. It will continue to firm up as it chills.

Q: How do I cut neat, professional-looking cheesecake slices?

Use a long, thin-bladed chef's knife. Dip the blade into a glass of hot water, wipe it clean with a paper towel, make a slice, and repeat. Cleaning and warming the blade between every cut ensures pristine results.

Q: How long can I store cheesecake in the refrigerator?

A fully baked cheesecake will keep beautifully in the refrigerator for up to 5 days. Wrap it tightly with plastic wrap or store it in an airtight container to prevent it from absorbing fridge odors.
